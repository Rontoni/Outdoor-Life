Welcome to nature!

In recent years the trend of adventuring out in nature has gone up in popularity. Especially in my homecountry of Sweden.
I myself use much of my sparetime visiting and living in nature recreationally.
That's where I and this site comes in, this will serve as an introduction for outdoor life in Sweden!

The Right Of Public Access

This is a law in Sweden that gives you the right to visit most nature areas, privatly owned forests and land!
What does this mean for us? As a tourist or a citizen of this country you can ride a bike, paddle canoes/kayaks, pick berries and camp almost wherever you want!
There are some exceptions and it also needs some common sense when used.
Things you shouldn't do:

  • Do not Destroy
  • Do not Litter
  • Do not Disturb

I will point out where most of these are applied in the camping and hiking sections!

The Swedish Tourist Association

This is a non-profit organization that works to make nature and culture accessible to everyone.


I'm mentioning them because they do excellent work and they are a vital factor for making tourists feel safe and cared for while wandering through the remote regions of Sweden.


If you want more information regarding the organization or too look for more activities, vist their website!
